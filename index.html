Projects

Micromouse Maze Solving Project

I contributed to a two-part project spanning hardware and software development. In the first phase, I designed and implemented the power subsystem onto a PCB, ensuring stable and efficient energy delivery for the system, with charging functionality. In the second phase, I utilized MATLAB’s SIMULINK to develop and implement control algorithms that enabled the micromouse to autonomously navigate and solve complex mazes. This end-to-end experience strengthened my skills in both circuit design and motion control programming, highlighting my ability to bridge hardware and software effectively.

Remote Penguin Pulse Oximeter

I engineered the power and location subsystems for a remote wildlife monitoring device. My role involved designing and implementing a reliable power management circuit capable of recharging, ensuring continuous operation. I integrated ESP32 microcontrollers to capture analog sensor data, convert it into a transmittable digital format, and facilitate remote data access. Additionally, I incorporated GPS tracking technology to enable real-time location monitoring. This project honed my skills in embedded systems design, wireless communication, and end-to-end development of practical, low-power electronic solutions.
